2.  **Limitations of Standard `malloc` for In-Memory Databases:**
    *   Standard `malloc` (often provided by the operating system's C library, e.g., `Glibc`) is the default mechanism for memory allocation in C.
    *   For in-memory databases like Redis, which perform frequent in-memory reads and writes, there is a high volume of heap data allocation and deallocation.
    *   This pattern often involves allocating and freeing many small objects (e.g., small keys and values).
    *   Operating systems manage memory in pages, typically 4KB (4096 bytes). When `malloc` requests memory, the OS may allocate a full page even if only a small portion is immediately needed.
    *   Frequent allocation and deallocation, especially of small, non-contiguous objects, can lead to **memory fragmentation**. This results in "vacuum spaces" where memory is free but cannot be easily used to satisfy new allocation requests, as it is too small or fragmented between allocated blocks.
